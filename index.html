Welcome!

Welcome to Guiding Eyes for the Blind at Cornell University. Whether you are an undergraduate student looking to join or a graduate student looking to relax with puppies, we are excited to show you all the club has to offer.

We are a student organization who collaborates with communities throughout upstate New York to raise, train, and bring guiding eyes dogs to all those in need. Since our inception, we are proud to have built a community dedicated to not only bringing superior guiding eyes dog to the blind, but bringing the Cornell community together. We hope to see you at our events soon!

About us

Guiding Eyes for the Blind at Cornell is a student-run organization that aims to raise, train, and nurture guiding eyes dogs for the visually impaired. Through accomplishing this mission, we not only hope to enhance communities throughout Upstate New York, but also help students learn more about guide dog training, disabilities, and mental health.

Get Involved

The best way to get involved is to come to our General Body Meetings, which are held four times a semester. Information about the date and location of these meetings are always sent to our email list and will be posted on our Facebook and Instagram a few days before each meeting. If you wish to be added to our mailing list to receive updates about meetings, events, and additional ways to get involved you can send an email to guidingeyes@cornell.edu requesting to be added. In order to be selected to become a puppy sitter, you need to be dedicated, come to meetings, events, puppy classes, and other opportunities that arise as the semester progresses.
